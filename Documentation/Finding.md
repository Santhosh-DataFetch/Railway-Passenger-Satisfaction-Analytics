## Key Finding 1: Service Quality Drives Satisfaction More Than Infrastructure Factors

Analysis of average service ratings revealed substantial differences between satisfied and dissatisfied passengers.

The strongest satisfaction drivers were:

1. Onboard Entertainment (+1.42)
2. Online Booking Ease (+1.13)
3. Online Support (+1.03)
4. Onboard Service (+0.89)
5. Online Boarding (+0.88)

In contrast, Platform Location showed no measurable difference between groups, suggesting that operational service quality has a greater influence on passenger satisfaction than station infrastructure factors.

These findings indicate that investments in customer experience and digital services may yield greater satisfaction improvements than investments in platform accessibility alone.


## Key Finding 2: Delays Are Associated With Lower Satisfaction

Passengers reporting lower satisfaction experienced higher average delays.

Average departure delay:

* Unsatisfied passengers: 17.74 minutes
* Satisfied passengers: 12.08 minutes

Average arrival delay:

* Unsatisfied passengers: 18.39 minutes
* Satisfied passengers: 12.20 minutes

These results indicate that punctuality contributes to passenger satisfaction. However, the magnitude of the delay differences appears smaller than the differences observed for several service-quality variables, suggesting that service experience may play a larger role in overall satisfaction.


## Key Finding 3: Customer Loyalty Is Strongly Associated With Satisfaction

Customer satisfaction differed substantially between loyal and disloyal passengers.

Satisfaction rates:

* Loyal customers: 61.6%
* Disloyal customers: 23.9%

Unsatisfied rates:

* Loyal customers: 38.4%
* Disloyal customers: 76.1%

This finding suggests that customer loyalty is one of the strongest factors associated with passenger satisfaction. Loyal customers may have greater trust in the service, more familiarity with travel processes, or a generally stronger relationship with the transportation provider.


## Key Finding 4: Business Travelers Report Higher Satisfaction Than Personal Travelers

Passenger satisfaction varied noticeably between travel purposes.

Satisfaction rates:

* Business Travel: 58.3%
* Personal Travel: 46.6%

Unsatisfied rates:

* Business Travel: 41.7%
* Personal Travel: 53.4%

Business travelers were approximately 11.7 percentage points more likely to report a positive travel experience than personal travelers.

This finding suggests that travel purpose influences passenger satisfaction. Business travelers may benefit from service offerings that better align with their needs, such as efficient booking processes, reliable service, and premium travel options. Further analysis is required to determine which specific service attributes contribute most to this difference.


## Key Finding 5: Digital Service Quality Is Strongly Associated With Passenger Satisfaction

Correlation analysis was conducted to identify variables most strongly associated with passenger satisfaction.

The strongest positive correlations were:

1. Onboard Entertainment (0.525)
2. Online Booking Ease (0.432)
3. Online Support (0.392)
4. Onboard Service (0.351)
5. Online Boarding (0.339)

A notable observation is that three of the top five factors are digital-service-related variables. This suggests that customer satisfaction is influenced not only by the onboard travel experience but also by the quality of digital interactions before and during the journey.

Platform Location showed no measurable relationship with satisfaction, while Arrival Time Convenience demonstrated a negligible correlation.


## Business Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the following actions are recommended:

1. Improve onboard entertainment services, as it is the strongest driver of passenger satisfaction.

2. Enhance digital services, including online booking, online support, and online boarding processes.

3. Reduce operational delays through improved scheduling and monitoring systems.

4. Develop targeted retention strategies for disloyal customers, who exhibit significantly lower satisfaction levels.

5. Continue focusing on business traveler experience while identifying opportunities to improve personal traveler satisfaction.
